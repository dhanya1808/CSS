Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of APJ Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 Born on 15th October Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 Graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1955 Enrolled at the MIT to study aerospace engineering
  • 1960 Joined DRDO as Chief Scientist
  • 1965 Started work rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1965 Started work rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 Transferred to ISRO
  • 1980-90 Responsible for the development of AGNI and PRITHVI Missiles
  • 1981 Padma Bhushan Government of India
  • 1990 Padma Vibhushan Government of India
  • 1992-99 Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the PM and the Secretary of DRDO
  • 1997 Bharat Ratna Government of India
  • 2002-07 Served as the 11th President of India
  • 2012 Launched mission for the youth of the nation called “What Can I Give Movement”
  • 2015 Passed away on 27 July in Shillong, Meghalaya
